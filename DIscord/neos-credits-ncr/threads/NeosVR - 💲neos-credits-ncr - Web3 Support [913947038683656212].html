Browsing this thread, its interesting and I have a few thoughts to add. The big area for me is the idea of digital rights, and how web3 and nft integration can present a new opportunity to reward creators. One challenge I forsee with digital rights is that there is a huge grey area. Like... say you own the digital rights to a pair of sunglasses. There's a common phrase about "your rights end where mine begin". So if you own the rights to use those sunglasses, does that mean I'm no longer allowed to make sunglasses that look like those? Will I be punished if I use a similar texture on my avatar to someone else's? Even if their avatar is literally just a small tweak to the base texture? There's no doubt in my mind that some people will expect that their one-of-a-kind cosmetic that they spent a lot for their avatar will actually be unique and that nobody else can use anything like that. And that seems scary to me. The nexus is all about post-scarcity so in many ways I feel like we should embrace that instead of trying to "fix" it.
At the end of the day there's a huge human factor to this that makes me uneasy, because that human factor has been abused with other content creation platforms like Youtube and the music industry. (edited)
Patent trolling is another example where digital rights really mess everything up.
