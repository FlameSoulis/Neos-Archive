That's an excellent question worth reflecting on for anyone holding NCR: why did polls around ending the ICO by token burns or staking (all things which could reasonably be expected to raise NCR price) generate ~$8M worth of sell pressure? I've been around in the community for over a year and a half so I think I have a decent read on what longer-term creatives and users are thinking. In short, many are absolutely furious. Not because of the possibility of the burn or staking, but because of the way this situation has been handled. Two polls were held which were sold as just gathering interesting datapoints, however a decision was then made immediately afterwards which makes the prior statements look pretty suspect. These polls were conducted in an almost information-free environment; the community hasn't been told why ending the ICO suddenly seems unrealistic, why it would be beneficial to happen now, what the consequences would be for longer-term Patrons & CDFT holders, how the system would even work ... etc. We were polled, apparently just to gather data, but without providing enough information for people to remotely understand what they were voting for and then what seems like a unilateral decision was made by Karel (i.e. even the other Neos team members didn't know a decision would be made this quickly). There appear to be some pretty serious failures of internal communication within the Neos team about this. To a cynical eye, it looks a lot like the polling was a pretext for manufacturing consent around a choice Karel already intended to make and we don't understand why. That's the reason so many people are pissed off about this and why there's been a bit of a crisis of confidence. (edited)
