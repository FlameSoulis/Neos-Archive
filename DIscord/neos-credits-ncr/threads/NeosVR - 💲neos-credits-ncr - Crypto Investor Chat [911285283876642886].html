Zyzyl 03-Dec-21 02:04 PM
There are many very significant differences between the Bitcoin and Ethereum networks. The biggest difference is that Ethereum is a smart contract platform - that allows for so much more than the Bitcoin network. Examples would be custom tokens (NCR), non-fungible tokens (NFTs), decentralised finance protocols... lots more besides. Another major difference is that Ethereum will be switching to a different method of handling new block creation - this involves a Proof of Stake process which has various advantages over Proof of Work method currently used by Ethereum & Bitcoin. In fairness, there are people who believe Proof of Stake has relevant disadvantages, though in my experience most of those people are heavily invested (monetarily & socially) in Bitcoin. There are pretty significant differences in the culture of the two groups as well IMO. As to whether it matters which is on top... it matters a lot to some people. Crypto can be a very tribal space. The price actually is important though. For Ethereum, once it goes Proof of Stake, the security of the network increases with a higher ETH price.
